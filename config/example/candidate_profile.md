# Sarah Ashley - Candidate Reference Handbook

## Who Sarah Is

Sarah Ashley is a Registered Nurse with seven years of experience, most of it on
busy hospital floors, who has grown into charge nurse and preceptor roles. She is
the nurse new hires get paired with because she explains the why behind the work
and stays calm when a unit gets slammed.

**Location:** Denver, Colorado, USA (open to relocation within the metro)
**Email:** sarah.ashley.example@gmail.com

## Career Timeline

### Front Range Medical Center (400-bed acute care hospital) | Charge Nurse, Med-Surg | 3 years
Promoted to charge nurse on a 32-bed medical-surgical unit. Runs shift
assignments, triages bed flow with the house supervisor, and is the go-to
resource for the floor. Serves as lead preceptor for new graduate nurses and
travelers, and sits on the unit's shared-governance practice council.

### Cherry Creek Community Hospital (mid-size community hospital) | 4 years
Progressively expanding roles:
- **Staff RN, Med-Surg / Telemetry** - cared for patients with complex,
  high-acuity needs and mentored junior nurses on the team.
- **Float Pool RN** - covered med-surg, tele, and post-surgical units, learning
  to ramp quickly into unfamiliar workflows.
- **New Graduate RN** - first role out of nursing school; completed the
  hospital's nurse residency program.

## Key Strengths

- **Direct patient care:** Seven years of hands-on med-surg and telemetry care.
  Strong on assessment, medication administration, and catching subtle changes
  in a patient's condition early.
- **Precepting & training:** Lead preceptor for new graduate nurses and
  travelers. Builds orientation checklists and walks new staff from nervous to
  independent.
- **Care coordination:** The connective tissue between physicians, case
  management, pharmacy, and families during discharge planning.
- **EHR proficiency:** Epic (super-user level), with experience optimizing unit
  documentation workflows and training peers.
- **Calm under pressure:** Trusted as charge nurse to keep a full unit running
  safely through admissions, codes, and short staffing.
- **Clear communication:** Translates clinical plans into plain language for
  patients and families, and into clean handoffs for the next shift.

## Key Gaps

- No formal management title (charge nurse, but not a unit manager with budget or
  hiring authority).
- No advanced practice licensure (not an NP or CNS).
- No ICU, ER, or OR specialty experience; background is med-surg and telemetry.
- No formal academic teaching experience (precepts at the bedside, has not taught
  in a nursing school).
- Single setting type (acute-care hospitals); limited clinic or community health
  experience.
